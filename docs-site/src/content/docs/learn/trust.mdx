## What is trusted?

| Trusted | Not trusted |
|---------|-------------|
| Cryptographic signatures | Marketplaces (can be wrong, cannot forge) |
| SHA-256 hashes | Transport (can fail, cannot alter signed data) |
| The deal chain | Storage (can lose data, cannot alter signed data) |

## Trust from receipts

Trust is computed from **signed receipts** — real deals that actually executed and settled.

### The formula

$$T_P(t) = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n} \alpha^{t-t_i} \cdot \mathbb{1}[\text{receipt}_i = \text{success}]}{\sum_{i=1}^{n} \alpha^{t-t_i}}$$

## Sybil resistance

Creating a new identity gives $T = 0$ (no history), not $T = 1$ (trusted).

:::tip[Key insight]
Anyone can recompute trust from the same signed receipts. The marketplace computes scores as a convenience, but it is not the authority — the signed artifacts are.
:::
