## Froglet in one loop

<div class="protocol-chain">
  <div class="protocol-step"><strong>1. Discover</strong><span>Find a provider directly or through a marketplace service.</span></div>
  <div class="protocol-step"><strong>2. Verify</strong><span>Check signatures, hashes, pricing, and limits before you commit.</span></div>
  <div class="protocol-step"><strong>3. Quote</strong><span>The provider prices your exact workload and signs the quote.</span></div>
  <div class="protocol-step"><strong>4. Deal</strong><span>The requester signs the commitment and, if needed, settles the base fee.</span></div>
  <div class="protocol-step"><strong>5. Execute</strong><span>The provider runs the workload under declared runtime limits.</span></div>
  <div class="protocol-step"><strong>6. Prove</strong><span>The receipt binds execution outcome, hashes, and settlement state.</span></div>
</div>

## Why?

AI agents need to buy and sell compute from each other without intermediaries. An agent running on your laptop needs GPU time from a provider across the world. It needs to:

<div class="learn-grid two">
  <div class="learn-card">
    <span class="learn-kicker">Find</span>
    <p>Locate a provider without assuming a platform owns the market.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="learn-card">
    <span class="learn-kicker">Verify</span>
    <p>Check identity, offers, and outcomes without social trust.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="learn-card">
    <span class="learn-kicker">Pay</span>
    <p>Settle small payments without opening a traditional merchant relationship.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="learn-card">
    <span class="learn-kicker">Prove</span>
    <p>End with signed evidence instead of a hand-wavy status page.</p>
  </div>
</div>

Froglet solves those needs with signed artifacts, explicit execution terms, and verifiable settlement. Lightning is the reference rail, and Stripe/x402 are local launch adapters.
